The DARPA Triage Challenge

The DARPA Triage Challenge is the latest in DARPA's robotic challenge series. The goal is to develop autonomous systems that can locate and triage casualties during mass casualty incidents, ranging from mass shootings to battlefields to natural disasters to large-scale transportation accidents.

Team PRONTO is one of four remaining DARPA-funded finalists in the DARPA Triage Challenge, based on our successful performance during the first two years of competitions. The final round of the competition will be held in November 2026.

Our Approach

The PRONTO system for
autonomous robotic triage

PRONTO integrates multiple aerial and ground robots to autonomously locate casualties, assess their injuries, measure vital signs remotely, and coordinate with human first responders. PRONTO collects and delivers this critical information to the first responders, enabling them to direct limited resources to save the most lives.

1. Locate casualties

Aerial robots rapidly locate casualties and perform initial injury assessments.

2. Measure vitals and injuries

Ground robots visit each casualty to collect detailed information on vitals and injuries.

3. Coordinate with first responders

Casualty reports are relayed to first responders in the field, directing them to the most critically injured.
