Our Robots

System Overview

Key Components

Aerial Quadrotors (up to 3)

Fly high and fast to provide an overview of the mission area, locating casualties and performing initial triage

Ground Robots (up to 7)

Provide detailed assessments of each casualty's vitals and injuries

Military-Grade Mesh Radio Network

Ensures robust communication among robots in austere environments

Laptop Base Station

Provides overall control and monitoring of the system, and aggregates reports for transmission to first responders

Mobile Devices for Medics

Displays locations of casualties, casualty assessments, and high-priority injuries to first responders

PRONTO Jackals

Robust wheeled robot with a long runtime

Built on top of a Clearpath Jackal base, these robots provide a stable platform for sensing vital signs and injuries. We outfit them with a custom sensor package, including a 3D LiDAR, and a custom onboard computer with a workstation-grade GPU.

Size

51 × 43 × 25 cm

Runtime

4 hours

Weight

17 kg

Max speed

2.0 m/s

Sensors

RGB, radiometric infrared, shotgun microphone, mmWave radar, event camera, 3D LiDAR, GPS+RTK

PRONTO Spots

Legged robots with expanded reach

We are expanding PRONTO to include legged robots for the 2026 competition. Our group has significant experience working with legged robots on multiple other projects, including DARPA SubT and DCIST. Adding Spot robots provide exciting new capablities, enabling traversal of stairs and cluttered environments. The Spots carry the same sensor package as the Jackals, but with expanded reach for optimal sensor positioning.

Size

110 x 50 x 61 cm

Runtime

90 minutes

Weight

34 kg

Max speed

1.6 m/s

Sensors

RGB, radiometric infrared, shotgun microphone, mmWave radar, event camera, 3D LiDAR, GPS+RTK

PRONTO Falcon Quadrotors

Versatile quadrotors built in-house at Penn

The Falcon platform is designed and built at the University of Pennsylvania. It is highly customizable and maneuverable, making it ideal for both research and field deployment.

Size

88 cm (diagonal, including propellers)

Runtime

40 minutes

Weight

4.2 kg (max takeoff weight)

Sensors

RGB, radiometric infrared, GPS+RTK, 3D lidar (optional)
